# Quadratic Formula and Equations

## Standard Form
A quadratic equation is of the form: ax² + bx + c = 0, where a ≠ 0.

## Quadratic Formula
x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / (2a)

## Discriminant
D = b² - 4ac
- D > 0: Two distinct real roots
- D = 0: One repeated real root (equal roots)
- D < 0: No real roots (complex conjugate roots)

## Common Mistakes
- Forgetting that a must be non-zero
- Sign errors when substituting into formula
- Not simplifying √(b²-4ac) when possible
